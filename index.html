UK Road Transport Greenhouse Gas Emissions 1990-2016

A greenhouse gas is a gas that absorbs and emits radiant energy within the thermal infrared range, causing the greenhouse effect. On Earth, the primary greenhouse gases within the atmosphere are water vapour, carbon dioxide, methane, nitrous oxide and ozone.

Greenhouse gases and the greenhouse effect are not neccessarily bad things. Without them, the temperature of the Earth's surface would be on average about -18°C (0°F), rather than the present average of 15°C (59°F). At such low temperatures, life on Earth would be vastly different if not impossible, and human society would likely have not come to exist.

However, since the dawn of the Industrial Revolution in around 1750, human activities have increased the atmospheric concentration of greenhouse gases. This is of concern, as the current scientific consensus holds that human (anthropogenic) greenhouse gas emissions will lead to a rise in average global temperatures over the next few decades, with potentially harmful effects on ecosystems, biodiversity, and the economy.

Road transport in particular is a major contributor to greenhouse gas emissions in many countries. In the UK, as of 2018 the majority of man-made greenhouse gas emissions come from road transport.
